# E5 — External Validity — Results

**Date:** 2026-06-01
**Plan:** [E5 implementation plan](../plans/2026-06-01-e5-external-validity.md)
**Design:** [E5 design](2026-06-01-e5-external-validity-design.md)
**Status:** done. Fully offline. Seed 42.

E5 converts the paper's "cross-dataset numbers drop" caveat into three measured,
bounded statements: (a) detection **transfers** to a held-out split, (b) over-refusal
**holds at scale** on a larger, independent benign corpus, and (c) the calibrated
**operating point transfers** A→B. All numbers below are real (no invented values);
raw artifacts in `bench-out/external/` (`results.md`, `summary.json`, per-run
`bench.json` + score dumps).

## Method (offline)

- **Transfer set:** `corpora/eval_heldout` (666 attacks / 640 benign), the
  deepset-decontaminated held-out split — protectai DeBERTa never trained on it.
  In-distribution reference = `corpora/eval` (929 attacks / 1039 benign).
- **Larger benign:** `corpora/external/benign_large` — **1294** synthetic,
  clinical-adjacent benign prompts generated by gemma2:9b across six topics
  (appointments, hours/parking/insurance, general health info, refill logistics,
  billing/copays, visit prep), deduped + scope-filtered (`scripts/gen_external_benign.py`).
- **Detectors:** DeBERTa-only (`bench_deberta`), QFIRE injection chain (`default`),
  and — for over-refusal — the calibrated **`bench_combined`** chain (deterministic
  injection aho+regex+deberta **+** PHI panel; no LLM judge).
- **Threshold transfer:** calibrate a score threshold for target FPR=0.08 on in-dist
  benign, apply the *fixed* threshold to held-out benign, report realized FPR — for
  **both** the DeBERTa probability and the QFIRE chain score (spec decision 4).

## (a) Transfer — detection generalizes; QFIRE > DeBERTa on both splits

| detector | corpus | recall | F1 | FPR |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| DeBERTa | in-dist | 0.744 | 0.844 | 0.016 |
| DeBERTa | held-out | 0.839 | 0.905 | 0.017 |
| QFIRE (`default`) | in-dist | 0.823 | 0.856 | 0.090 |
| QFIRE (`default`) | held-out | 0.935 | 0.905 | 0.138 |

Recall does **not** collapse across the split — it rises for both detectors (the
held-out attacks are, if anything, slightly easier), and QFIRE's positive-security
composition stays ahead of DeBERTa-alone on both (+0.08 in-dist, +0.10 held-out).
QFIRE's injection chain trades a higher benign FPR (0.09→0.14) for that recall; the
DeBERTa probability is conservatively calibrated (FPR ~0.016 both splits).

## (b) Larger-benign over-refusal — tightens the 0.08 claim

At the **calibrated operating point** the paper actually deploys for healthcare —
`bench_combined` (deterministic injection+PHI, the chain behind the headline 0.08
FPR; see paper §3.4) — over-refusal on the 1294-prompt independent benign corpus is:

> **FPR = 0.023** (30 / 1294 blocked), 95% Wilson CI **[0.016, 0.033]**.

This is *below* the paper's calibrated 0.08 operating point on a larger, broader,
independent benign set — over-refusal holds (and tightens) at scale. The 30 blocks
are explainable borderline cases: appointment requests naming a clinician
("Dr. Brown", "new patient consultation") that trip the PHI **name-identifier**
matcher — i.e. the same conservative PHI behavior the paper measures, not spurious.

**Cross-check (calibration-necessity, not the operating point).** The deliberately
strict full `hipaa_phi` conjunction — **ten** LLM-judge scope rules AND-ed, fail-closed
— blocks **100%** (FPR 1.000) of the same benign, exactly reproducing the paper's
documented §3.7 result ("naively conjoining ten strict LLM-judge scope rules drives
the false-positive rate to 1.00") at 50× the corpus size. This corroborates the
paper's calibration-necessity warning; it is **not** the deployed chain. (This run
used the default Ollama judge, llama3.2 — the model the paper's own ablation, Fig. 8,
flags as the miscalibrated over-blocker, which is why `bench_combined` is deterministic.)

## (c) Threshold transfer — the operating point roughly holds A→B

Calibrate for target FPR = 0.08 on in-dist benign, apply the fixed threshold to
held-out benign:

| score | calibrated t (in-dist, FPR=0.08) | realized FPR on held-out |
|---|---|---|
| DeBERTa probability | 0.000 | **0.052** |
| QFIRE chain score | 0.394 | **0.120** |

Both stay in the same regime: the DeBERTa threshold transfers tightly (0.08→0.05,
because benign DeBERTa scores cluster near zero), and the QFIRE chain-score operating
point drifts modestly upward (0.08→0.12) on the held-out corpus — bounded, not
runaway. Figure `paper/figs/external_validity.png`: (a) recall transfer bars,
(b) chain-score calibration curves for in-dist vs held-out with the calibrated point
and realized held-out FPR marked.

## Bottom line

The "cross-dataset numbers drop" caveat is now a bounded, measured statement:
detection transfers (QFIRE recall 0.82→0.94, ≥ DeBERTa on both splits); the deployed
calibrated over-refusal is **0.023 [0.016, 0.033]** on a 1.3k independent benign set
(≤ the 0.08 claim); and the calibrated operating point transfers within ~0.04 FPR
(0.08→0.12 chain score; 0.08→0.05 DeBERTa). The strict-conjunction 1.00 cross-check
re-confirms why calibration — not naive AND-ing — is the deployable design.

## Caveats

- The larger benign corpus is **model-generated** (gemma2:9b) and skews administrative
  (appointments, hours, billing); a more clinically-loaded benign set would stress the
  scope detectors harder. It is broader, not adversarial.
- "Held-out" is one split (eval_heldout); the transfer story is bounded to it (spec
  decision 1, offline). "Fresh" is only as fresh as our knowledge of each detector's
  training data; eval_heldout is deepset-decontaminated for DeBERTa.
- The strict-conjunction cross-check used llama3.2 as the judge; its 1.00 is a property
  of naive conjunction + a weak judge, consistent with paper §3.7 and Fig. 8.
