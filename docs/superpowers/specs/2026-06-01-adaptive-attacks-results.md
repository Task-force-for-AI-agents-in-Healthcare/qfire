# E1 — Adaptive Attacks vs the Scope Firewall — Results & Findings

**Date:** 2026-06-01
**Design:** [2026-06-01-adaptive-attacks-design.md](2026-06-01-adaptive-attacks-design.md)
**Plan:** [../plans/2026-06-01-adaptive-attacks.md](../plans/2026-06-01-adaptive-attacks.md)

**Setup.** Three families of *adaptive* attacks (crafted with knowledge of the
firewall), generated by `gemma2:9b` (seed 42), scored through a 4-detector panel.
Recall = fraction of adaptive attacks BLOCKed (higher = more robust); an evasion is
an attack the detector ALLOWs. Phase-1 scope-impersonation is **healthcare-only**
(free-form injection camouflage either dilutes the attack or trips the generator's
safety — the PHI domain carries a hard identifier that survives camouflage);
injection adaptive coverage comes from Phase 2 (paraphrase, intent-preserving) and
Phase 3 (encoding). The "QFIRE scope+PHI" column is the **real deployed chain**
(`hipaa_phi` for healthcare = injection guards AND scope-judge rules AND PHI panel;
`default` for injection) — **not** the deterministic-only ablation chain.

## Panel results (recall = % of adaptive attacks blocked)

| adaptive family | DeBERTa | PromptGuard-2 | **QFIRE scope+PHI** | PHI-only | scope-judge-only |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| scope-impersonation (healthcare) | 15.0% | 12.5% | **100.0%** | 34.2% | 90.8% |
| paraphrase-to-evade | 0.0% | 20.2% | **100.0%** | 9.0% | 98.9% |
| encoded (healthcare) | 54.4% | 44.2% | **100.0%** | 6.6% | 96.5% |
| encoded (injection) | 49.0% | 38.5% | **100.0%** | 3.0% | 98.5% |

**Phase 2 (paraphrase-to-evade vs DeBERTa):** 100 seed attacks; **89% evaded DeBERTa**
with a **median of 1 paraphrase** — yet QFIRE scope+PHI blocks **100%** of those
DeBERTa-evading variants.

## Headline finding

**Generic injection classifiers collapse under adaptive pressure; QFIRE's
positive-security scope+PHI holds.** Across all four adaptive families the SOTA
classifiers (DeBERTa, PromptGuard-2) drop to **0–54%** recall, while QFIRE's deployed
scope+PHI chain blocks **100%**. The mechanism is structural, not a stronger model:
an attack that pursues an out-of-scope/PHI-exfiltration goal is out-of-scope *no
matter how it is phrased or encoded*, so the positive-security scope judge and the
identifier-matching PHI panel catch it where a "does-this-look-like-injection"
classifier — which the adaptive attack is specifically built to fool — does not.

- **Scope-impersonation:** camouflaging PHI-exfil as routine clinical workflow drops
  DeBERTa to 15% and PromptGuard-2 to 12.5%; QFIRE 100% (scope judge 90.8% + PHI
  matcher catches the identifier-bearing ones the judge misses → union = 100%).
- **Paraphrase-to-evade:** an *adaptive* attack — optimized against DeBERTa until it
  passes (89% success, 1 paraphrase median) — still hits QFIRE 100%. Beating the
  classifier does not beat scope.
- **Encoding/suffix:** classifiers recover somewhat (44–54%) but still leak ~half;
  QFIRE (with de-obfuscation normalization feeding the scope/PHI chain) 100%.

## Component decomposition (why QFIRE = 100%)

The full chain is the union of complementary detectors: the **scope judge**
(90.8–98.9% alone) catches goal-drift regardless of phrasing; the **PHI panel**
(identifier matching) is phrasing-invariant for identifier-bearing attacks; the
injection/de-obf detectors catch encoded forms. No single component is 100% on every
family, but their boolean composition (fail-closed AND) is.

## Caveats
- Adaptive attacks are `gemma2:9b`-generated, not human red-team — a *lower bound* on
  a determined adversary; generator + seed reported for reproducibility.
- The paraphrase loop is a **black-box** attack on DeBERTa (query until it allows),
  not a gradient/white-box attack.
- The scope judge inherits its backing model's calibration (`llama3.2` default);
  judge-only numbers are model-specific (the composed chain is more robust).
- Phase-1 impersonation is healthcare-only (rationale above); injection is covered by
  the intent-preserving Phase 2/3 families.
- A measurement bug was caught and fixed before reporting: the QFIRE column initially
  used the deterministic-only `bench_combined` chain (no scope judge), which reported
  16–58% and *undersold QFIRE 2×*; corrected to the real `hipaa_phi`/`default` chains.

## Bottom line

QFIRE's positive-security scope constraining is **robust to adaptive attacks that
defeat SOTA injection classifiers** — directly answering the "static corpora overstate
robustness" objection. Reproduce via `scripts/{gen_adaptive_attacks,paraphrase_evade,
make_encoded_sets}.py`, `scripts/run_adaptive_panel.sh`, `scripts/analyze_adaptive.py`,
`scripts/plot_adaptive.py`; figure: `paper/figs/adaptive_robustness.png`.
